# Darwin's Canvas

Darwin's Canvas is a procedural ecosystem simulator where you can create, observe, and interact with a living digital terrarium. It gives you a blank canvas to build a food chain from scratch and see how different creatures interact with each other in real-time. 

You can take a hands-off approach and just watch the simulation run, or you can intervene directly by using various tools to shape the environment. 

## How to Play

When you launch the game, you'll start with an empty environment.

1. **Create and Populate:** Use the Creation Lab to spawn your first creatures. You can mix herbivores, carnivores, and omnivores to establish a food web.
2. **Observe:** You can pan around and zoom in to track specific creatures as they roam, hunt for food, sleep, and level up. 
3. **Interact:** If you want to intervene, use the toolbar at the bottom of the screen. You can drop food for starving creatures, heal them, lure them to specific spots, grab them, or smite predators if the population gets out of hand.

## Core Features

- **Ecosystem Simulation:** Creatures operate independently based on their traits. They get hungry, hunt, sleep, and grow. The food chain plays out dynamically based on what you put into the world.
- **God Tools:** You can physically interact with the simulation by spawning custom creatures or using powers like Smite, Heal, Feed, Lure, and Grab.
- **Day/Night Cycles:** The environment transitions through dawn, day, dusk, and night, which affects visibility and creature behavior.
- **Hybrid Audio Engine:** The game features a custom audio system with dynamic background music that crossfades based on the time of day, alongside spatial audio for sound effects depending on where your camera is looking.
